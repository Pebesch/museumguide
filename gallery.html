Project goal
Rewrite Storytelling

Our goal was to help the Dojo team create management software for their new product: The Dojo stick.

This innovative IoT device functiones as an audio guide that emits sound by bone conduction. However the product is still under development and it needs software to manage and prepare exhibitons to later upload them to the device itself.

That's where the FHNW students team helped the Dojo team to develop the userinterface and -experience, as well as parts of the software. The software was to consist of 4 parts:

  • Dojo API
  • Exhibitor Management
  • Exhibtion Management
  • Kiosk Terminal

We ended up building the Dojo API and the Exhibiton Management and designing the UI/UX of the Kiosk Terminal using Figma. This restriction was due to the Covid-19 situation shuting down IP3.

Dojo API

The Dojo API serves as the single point of truth for all dojo related data. All other software components were to access this API to display and manipulate data.

Exhibition Management

The Exhibition Management is the software used by exhibitors to create and manage exhibtions. These exhibtions consist of multiple exhibits that each have separate audio tracks and information.

Kiosk Terminal

The Kiosk Terminal is the software used by exhibitions visitors. A visitor will check-in using a touch device. He will select a tour for which he will be guided by the audio guide.

When leaving the exhibtion he will check-out and receive a summary of all content he bookmarked on the dojo stick during his stay.

Initial situation

We started the project without source material to begin with.

Most artefacts were created with the client in separate focus meetings. The UI/UX team and the developer team worked in parallel to gain the most momentum.

The dev team started by setting up the repositories, initializing the tech stack and developing the first iteration of the database scheme

The UI/UX team developed the Exhibition Management with the client as Figma prototype, so that the developers were able to start implementing it, when IP4 begann. While the Exhibition Management was being constructed the UI/UX team sketched the Kiosk Terminal together with the customer.

Benefits of your solution

Because of the dojo stick still being under construction and the startup being founded during the project time the scope and needs for the software were prone to change.

Our modular approach and splitting the concerns into different small applications enabled us to react to change rapidly.

No mather how the business is going to change, the API will be able to change with it and provide it's functionality to each application connected.

By keeping the user interfaces lean and bound to one purpose they can easily be adapted to match any change that is about to happen.

Since the UI/UX team worked closely with the customer we are certain that we built the right application and prepared them to showcase their goal to a potential exhibitor.

Results
Dojo API

The API was designed to meet all endpoints needed in the Exhibition Managment. This includes endpoints for CRUD operations, find and findAll methods.

Exhibtion Mangement
  • CRUD operations on exhibitions
  • CRUD operations on exhibits
  • I18n support for the application and it's content
  • Storing and reading files from the Google Cloud service

Our client asked us to put more time and effort into a clickable Figma prototype for the Exhibition Management to showcase new features and UI/UX elements they came up with in the later stages of IP4.

Kiosk Terminal

In order to showcase the vision and check -in and -out experience, we developed a clickable prototype with our customer to showcase the whole functionality to a potential exhibitor.
